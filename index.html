Japanese PM Kishida Prefers Refreshing at Chain Salons

Personal Care of the Heads of Government: A Glimpse into Their Daily Routines from the Article.

By Shotaro Miyasaka | July 22, 2023 | Data Sources are Available in Github | Back to Homepage

Being the Prime Minister is undoubtedly one of the busiest professions, with schedules planned to the minute. In Japan, major news media have diligently recorded the activity logs of successive Prime Ministers, detailing whom they met, where, and at what time. It is rare worldwide to find daily schedules of government leaders documented to this extent, making these records a valuable source for understanding their realities.

Given the nature of their responsibilities, Prime Ministers' health and appearance are crucial considerations. By analyzing the "Shusho-Kantei"(PM's office) articles published in the daily newspaper, Nikkei intriguing data has emerged, offering insight into the personal care habits of each Prime Minister.

The incumbent PM, Fumio Kishida, took office on October 4, 2021. According to the data, he has been getting a haircut at an average frequency of approximately once every two weeks since assuming office. In less than 19 months up to the end of June this year, he visited the barber shop 41 times, without any gaps of one month or more.

This frequency is twice as high as his predecessors, Yoshihide Suga and Shinzo Abe. Both Abe and Suga, during their tenures, got haircuts at a rate of about once a month.

Kishida seems to prefer visiting Hair Mode Kikuchi Kanda-Nichigin-dori Street branch , located in Tokyo's business district. Of the 41 times, he initially went to a different shop for the first nine visits, but the most recent 32 occasions have all been at this shop, located near the Bank of Japan's headquarters.

Hair Mode Kikuchi is a high-end barber chain with three locations in Tokyo. Of the first nine visits, Kishida used the same chain's salon, Hair Mode Kikuchi Ginza branch. This sets him apart from Suga, who consistently got haircuts at a salon in Nagatacho, near the Prime Minister's official residence and the National Diet.

Additionally, it appears that Kishida enjoys massages. Since taking office, he has visited the foot massage shop "Queensway Ginza Namikidori" nine times until the end of June this year. Queensway is a chain of "British-style reflexology" stores with branches in major cities across Japan.

Each Prime Minister has their unique way of refreshing themselves. Shinzo Abe, for instance, was known for his love of golf and even played with then-U.S. President Donald Trump on occasion. Meanwhile, Yoshihide Suga made morning walks a daily routine.

Kishida will celebrate his 66th birthday on July 29th. If all goes as planned, he will complete two years in office in this October. The value of his routine health management will undoubtedly be scrutinized as he continues in his role.
